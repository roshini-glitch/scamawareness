Love scams target victims by building fake romantic relationships online, gaining trust and eventually requesting money. These scammers usually use dating apps, social media, or gaming platforms to gain your trust.

Red Flag: They do not want your heart, they want your wallet.

How Love Scammers Operate

They build trust

Daily sweet messages, calls, or even video chats to make you believe it's real.

They create emotional connection

They quickly express deep love and future plans, often too soon.

Red Flags to Watch For

  • They say they "love you" over the phone quickly, often within days or weeks.
  • They avoid face to face meetings, even if they claim to be in Singapore.
  • They often claim to be overseas, working in the military, oil rigs, or foreign business.
  • They try to isolate you from friends or family who raise concerns.
  • They ask for money, sometimes repeatedly, for emergencies, debts, or travel.
