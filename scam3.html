Check for Red flags

Fraudsters employ advanced methods to convince you to give up your cash. You can look for warning signs to avoid falling victim to investment scams

Check for the Company Background

Don't believe everything you are told too quickly.

Check the entity's or the investment opportunity's details, including the representatives' backgrounds and the entity's performance history.

Asking the business as many questions as necessary to completely comprehend the investment opportunity is also crucial.

Be cautious if it fails to respond to your enquiries or sidesteps them.

Real stories:

Victim 1:

"A scammer contacted an online trader specializing in binary options, cryptocurrency, and forex trading, promising guaranteed returns. The trader convinced the investor to invest more, expecting better returns. However, when the investor wanted to withdraw their money, they were told they needed to pay taxes on their profits. The trader pressured the investor to increase their volume, threatening to lose everything unless they invested more. The scammer felt embarrassed and knew the scam was fake."

Source: scamwatch.gov.au

Victim 2:

"Scammers would build rapport with victims to gain their trust before introducing them to "investment opportunities", which would sometimes involve cryptocurrencies. Victims would be asked to open accounts at crypto exchanges and transfer money into the accounts to purchase cryptocurrencies. They are then tricked into transferring the cryptocurrencies to fraudulent cryptocurrency trading platforms or the scammers' own wallets. In some cases, victims would initially receive small profits from the scammer to induce them to continue investing. Fake websites or applications displaying a purported rise in profits would also lead victims to invest larger sums, the police said.."
