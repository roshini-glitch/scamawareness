Investment Scam

Investment scams lure victims with promises of high returns through fake or misleading investment schemes.

How It Works

  • Scammers offer 'too good to be true' investment opportunities.
  • They pressure victims to invest quickly without proper paperwork.
  • Victims lose money when the scam collapses or the scammers disappear.

How to Avoid

  • Research investments thoroughly before committing funds.
  • Verify legitimacy with regulatory bodies such as MAS (Monetary Authority of Singapore).
  • Never rush decisions or pay via untraceable methods.

Real Story:

A man lost $150,000 in a fake cryptocurrency investment promoted via social media influencers. Always do your due diligence.
