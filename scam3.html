üí∞ What's an Investment Scam?

Fraudsters promise guaranteed high returns with little to no risk. They use fake testimonials, pressure tactics, and sophisticated platforms to steal your money. Remember: if it sounds too good to be true, it probably is!

Red Flag: Reacting fast after falling prey to a scam is crucial. This gives you a chance to recover your funds or prevent further losses.

Essential Insights

All investments carry risks. Always examine before you invest! Check for red flags and be cautious of opportunities that offer profits at little or no risk.

Verify that the company and its representatives are regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS).

Out of the blue, you might be added to a group chat where people share their jackpot from a "not-to-be-missed" investment. Or you're contacted on social media about a profitable investment opportunity. It could even be an ad praising profitable "investment opportunities."

Check for Red Flags

Guaranteed High Returns

Promises of profits with little to no risk. Legitimate investments always carry some risk.

Pressure to Act Fast

Limited-time offers that push you to invest immediately without time to research.

Referral Commissions

Legitimate investment schemes generally don't offer commissions for bringing in friends.

Unregulated Companies

Companies not supervised by MAS or other legitimate financial authorities.

Real Story 1

"A scammer contacted an online trader specializing in binary options, cryptocurrency, and forex trading, promising guaranteed returns. The trader convinced the investor to invest more, expecting better returns. However, when the investor wanted to withdraw their money, they were told they needed to pay taxes on their profits. The scammer pressured the investor to increase their volume, threatening to lose everything unless they invested more."

Real Story 2

"Scammers built rapport with victims to gain trust before introducing 'investment opportunities' involving cryptocurrencies. Victims opened accounts at crypto exchanges and transferred money to purchase cryptocurrencies. They were then tricked into transferring the cryptocurrencies to fraudulent trading platforms. In some cases, victims initially received small profits to induce them to continue investing larger sums."

Got Scammed?

Call your bank immediately and make a police report. Contact the ScamShield Helpline at 1799. The faster you act, the better chance of getting help and preventing further losses.
