Someone calls, texts, or emails pretending to be someone you trust. Could be the police, your bank, even your own family. Their goal? Get your money or personal info by making you panic.

Red Flag: They always want you to act RIGHT NOW. Don't fall for the pressure!

Common Scammer Tactics

Fake Police Call

"You're under investigation. Transfer money to clear your name."

Government Agency

Pretending to be from MOM, ICA, or other official bodies.

Bank "Verification"

"We need to verify suspicious transactions on your account."

Hacked Friend

Using your friend's social media to ask for emergency money.

How Impersonation Scams Work

  • Scammers call victims claiming to be from the police, bank, or government agencies
  • They create fake urgency, warning victims of crimes, fraud, or account issues
  • Victims are pressured to act immediately without time to think
  • They're asked to transfer money or provide banking details and passwords
  • Scammers may use spoofed numbers to appear legitimate

How to Avoid Being Scammed

  • Never disclose personal information, bank details, or passwords over the phone
  • Always verify caller identity independently by calling official numbers
  • Be suspicious of urgent demands for money transfers or immediate action
  • Remember that real authorities will never ask you to transfer money
  • Take time to think - legitimate calls can wait
  • If in doubt, hang up and call back using official contact numbers

Real Example 1

Sarah got a call from "police" saying her account was linked to money laundering. Scared, she transferred $10,000 to a "secure account" for investigation. It was all fake. Don't be Sarah.

Real Example 2

Michael received a call from someone claiming to be from his bank, saying his account was compromised. They asked for his PIN and OTP to "secure" his account. He shared the details and lost $5,000 within hours. Always verify independently!

Got Scammed?

Report it immediately through ScamShield app or contact police. The faster you act, the better chance of getting help.
