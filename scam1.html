Impersonation Scam Aarthi

Impersonation scams involve scammers pretending to be someone trustworthy, such as government officers or bank officials, to trick victims into giving money or personal information.

How It Works

  • Scammers call victims claiming to be from the police or bank.
  • They create fake urgency, warning victims of crimes or fraud.
  • Victims are asked to transfer money or provide banking details.

How to Avoid

  • Do not disclose personal or bank details over the phone.
  • Verify caller identity independently by calling official numbers.
  • Be suspicious of urgent demands for money transfers.

Real Story:

A 45-year-old professional lost $50,000 after scammers impersonated police officers, claiming his identity was stolen. Always verify suspicious calls independently.
